Tamil Tigers

The early 1990s. In Sri Lanka, three powerful sides are facing off, and the stakes are high. The Tamil Tigers, a separatist organization, are at the peak of their influence, with the support of a significant portion of Sri Lankans behind them. The Sri Lankan government is in a desperate struggle to defeat the rebels and preserve unity. India is backing and funding the Sri Lankan government's resistance to the Tamil Tigers, despite internal pressure. The stability of the entire region is in jeopardy. How will you help your side succeed?

European Union

Begun as an effort to strengthen the region's economies, the European Union appeared to accomplish the impossible: bringing together feuding nations after centuries of near-constant unrest. Though the opening of borders and the unification of currency strengthened Europe, they left it with a host of new problems. The financial crisis in Ireland and Greece has sent ripples across the continent, as nations try to come to grips with high unemployment and soaring debt. Unrest in North Africa tops headlines and is fueling immigration concerns. The situation seems to be waiting but for a spark. How you as a leader deal with these and other looming crises will determine the fate of the EU.

Committee

  • Council of the European Union

Directors

Sino-Indian Conflict: 2022

As a result of decades of growth, China and India have become the second and third largest economies of the world, respectively. Their political clout and global influence have grown. At the same time, both countries are rivals, competing for influence, resources, and economic domination. Both have militarized significantly. Any conflict that breaks out will send the financial markets and the international community into turmoil. The United States, focused on maintaining its global influence and global stability, often finds itself as the fulcrum balancing both nations. Can China and India continue to grow peacefully or will their competing interests bring them to conflict? To what extent will the U.S. and Pakistan be able to act as the balance between the two nations? You will decide.

  • India
  • United States
  • Pakistan

Directors

Iranian Council

Iran today faces numerous internal and external pressures. Unrest among students has flared up continuously over the last few years, while the United States, along with the international community, is strengthening sanctions against Iran. Even naval movements within its own territorial waters has garnered international attention. The Iranian government must put its own house in order and address the hostility Iran faces in the international community. How clerical leaders like the Ayatollah and political leaders like President Ahmadinejad confront these challenges to maintain a balance between the Koran and secular modernization will determine what role the Islamic Republic will play in a rapidly changing Middle East and West Asia.

Committee

  • Guardian Council of the Constitution

Men, Money and Munitions

Take a seat on the executive board of one of the most powerful defense contractors in the world. These companies not only develop and protect some of the most valuable defense secrets science has to offer, but also manage billions of dollars in investment while carefully situated to capitalize on conflict and despair. Managing a company like this is a remarkable balancing act, but what happens when it all goes awry? You and your board will navigate through scandal, espionage, fraud and more to ultimately determine whether you, your company and your country will survive.
